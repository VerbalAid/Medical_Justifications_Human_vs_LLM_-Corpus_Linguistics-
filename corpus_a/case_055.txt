Acute urticaria: characterized by erythematous-edematous, evanescent, pruritic, evanescent lesions, lasting less than 24 hours, without desquamation.

The general condition is usually preserved.

In children the annular pattern is more frequent.

In both rubella and toxicoderma, the general condition is not preserved.

In staphylococcal shock, the general condition is affected and the lesions are blistering.

Scabies lesions are preferably interdigital, in the form of papulocoses that can follow linear trajectories.
