You are asking about auriculotemporal syndrome or Frey's syndrome.

It is the clinical expression of a vegetative neuropathy caused by mechanical or irritative lesion of the vegetative fibers of the auriculotemporal nerve in its infratemporal pathway.

In our environment the most frequent cause is surgery of the split gland with a percentage of occurrence between 10 and 80%.

Conservative treatment with botulinum toxin offers good results.
