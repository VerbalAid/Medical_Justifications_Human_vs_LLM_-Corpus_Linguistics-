The question is tricky: it speaks of a burn of 10% of the body surface and the treatment of choice for second degree burns less than 20% of the BSA should be topical and monitored.

However, there is a warning sign: there is no arterial pulse and the burn is circumferential to the limb.

This is an emergency that, if allowed to evolve, will compromise the viability of the affected limb and end in amputation: an emergency escharotomy must be performed in order to relieve the pressure of the third space on the arterial vascular trunk.
