Having antibodies against the core implies natural contact, and that it is IgG, which is not acute.

The absence of e antigen rules out active replication.

And the persistence of surface antigen (HBsAg) and virus DNA indicates that it is still present.

Together, these indicate an asymptomatic carrier.

Carriers of the pre-core mutant usually have recurrent symptomatic periods and increased liver inflammation.

There would be insufficient data to rule it out completely, but it is not among the answers and indicates a completely normal physical examination.
