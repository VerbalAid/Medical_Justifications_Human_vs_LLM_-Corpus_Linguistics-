The most likely diagnosis in this case is primary hyperaldosteronism (PHA), also known as Conn's syndrome. This condition is characterized by aldosterone excess due to overproduction by one or both adrenal glands (usually unilateral), leading to hypertension, hypokalemia, and metabolic alkalosis.
