Diagnostic Suspicion: Amyloidosis (Option 3)

The presented case suggests a renal disorder characterized by edema, hypogammaglobulinemia, and specific proteinuria, with the predominance of kappa light chains. The patient's creatinine level of 5 mg/dL, anemia (10 g/dL), and hypogammaglobulinemia, particularly the deficiency in IgG, IgA, and IgM, support this suspicion.

Urinalysis revealing the presence of kappa light chains further supports the diagnosis of amyloidosis, a disorder characterized by extracellular deposition of abnormal protein fibrils, predominantly amyloid A (AA) or light chains (AL), leading to organ dysfunction.

While other differentials such as Kappa light chain deposition disease (Option 1) and light chain myeloma (Option 5) might also present with renal involvement, they typically do not exhibit the marked hypogammaglobulinemia, anemia, and nephrotic-range proteinuria observed in this case.

Nephrotic syndrome (Option 2) can cause nephrotic-range proteinuria, but it is generally characterized by normal serum immunoglobulins, and the absence of monoclonal proteinuria
