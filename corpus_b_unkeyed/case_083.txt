The most likely diagnosis in this case is Exudative Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD). The presence of abundant hard exudates, deep hemorrhages, and a localized neurosensory retinal detachment in the affected eye, coupled with the finding of abundant soft drusen in the contralateral eye, aligns with the features of exudative AMD. Although Acute Posterior Vitreous Detachment (APVD) may also present with vitreous detachment, the presence of hard exudates, hemorrhages, and retinal detachment suggests a more pathological process, such as AMD. Central Retinal Artery Obstruction (CRAO) typically presents with sudden, painless, and profound visual loss, with an afflicted optic disc appearing pale and swollen.
