The most probable initial diagnosis appears to be Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA), specifically the granulomatous variant. The patient's symptoms, such as asthenia, febrile fever, nasal obstruction, and mucus emission with clots, coupled with the presence of erythematous mucosa in the nostrils and proptosis of the right eyeball, are suggestive of nasal and ocular involvement, which are common in GPA. The presence of an elevated ESR and microhematuria with proteinuria further support this diagnosis, although these findings can also be seen in other ANCA-associated vasculitides.
