1- The patient's decreased growth, sexual development, and delayed bone age, combined with the onset of headaches and visual problems, suggest a hormonal deficit. The involvement of the optic chiasm, as indicated by the left temporal partial hemianopsia, supports a diagnosis of a hypothalamic-pituitary disorder, such as a hypothalamic tumor, craniopharyngioma, or pituitary adenoma. The low levels of gonadotrophins (FSH and LH) and estradiol further support this hypothesis, as these hormones are secreted by the pituitary gland and are crucial for sexual development. The absence of polydipsia and polyuria does not rule out a hypothalamic tumor, as these symptoms may not always be present
