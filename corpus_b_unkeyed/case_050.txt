This presentation suggests a common perioperative complication in elderly patients—delirium, which is often precipitated by stress, pain, and medications, particularly in those with a history of dementia.

While all options may have some merit in managing this complex patient, option 1—Delaying surgery until the confusional picture has disappeared—is the most appropriate choice. Delirium can increase the risk of complications during surgery and prolonged hospital stay, and it is often reversible if the underlying cause is addressed. Therefore, optimizing the patient's mental status prior to surgery would be prudent.
