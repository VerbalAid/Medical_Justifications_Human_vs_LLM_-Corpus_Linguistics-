In pregnant women, Escherichia coli is the most frequent culprit in urinary tract infections (UTIs). While other bacteria such as Enterococcus faecalis, Streptococcus agalactiae, Proteus mirabilis, and Staphylococcus saprophyticus can also cause UTIs, E. coli accounts for approximately 80-90% of all cases in this population. The patient's presentation, including left lumbar pain and dysuria, along with the urinalysis findings, supports E. coli as the most likely causative agent.
