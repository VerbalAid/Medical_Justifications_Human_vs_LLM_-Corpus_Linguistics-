Based on the presented symptoms of food impaction at the retrosternal level with difficulty swallowing saliva, the most likely diagnosis is Eosinophilic esophagitis (EE). This allergic-inflammatory disorder is characterized by eosinophilic infiltration of the esophageal mucosa, leading to symptoms such as food impaction and dysphagia. The patient's history of asthma, allergies to pollens, mites, and cat hair further support this diagnosis, as EE often presents with a background of atopic diseases.
