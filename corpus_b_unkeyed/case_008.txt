The diagnostic tests most useful for this case are likely 3- Biopsy of subcutaneous cellular tissue and 4- Determination of anti-smooth muscle antibodies. A skin biopsy can help confirm the presence of leptospirosis or dermatomyositis, both conditions associated with violaceous lesions and muscle weakness. Anti-smooth muscle antibodies are specific markers for autoimmune myositis, a group of disorders that can cause muscle weakness and skin changes similar to those described. The other tests (1-Determination of serum aldolase and 2- Electroencephalogram) are less relevant given the primary symptoms presented. As for 5- Genetic study of their descendants, this is not indicated in the initial evaluation of a single patient with these symptoms.
