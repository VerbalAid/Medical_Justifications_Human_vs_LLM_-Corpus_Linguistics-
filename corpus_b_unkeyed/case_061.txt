Given the patient's history of COPD, recent influenza, and current immunosuppression due to corticosteroid therapy, as well as the presence of skin abscesses, central facial paralysis, fever, and apical infiltrates with pleural effusion on chest X-ray, I would lean towards a diagnosis of **4- Aspergillosis**. This fungal infection can present with pulmonary and extrapulmonary manifestations, including skin lesions and neurological complications such as facial paralysis (Ramakrishnan et al., 2013). The patient's persistent cough with purulent and occasionally hemoptotic expectoration, febrile fever, anorexia, asthenia, and weight loss further support this diagnosis.
