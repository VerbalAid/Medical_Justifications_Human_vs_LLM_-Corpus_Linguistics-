The presented case exhibits a constellation of findings strongly suggesting Goodpasture's disease, a form of rapid progressive glomerulonephritis (RPGN). The patient's high creatinine and urea levels, along with a positive titer of anti-GBM antibodies, are characteristic of this disorder.

The renal biopsy findings of crescentic glomerulonephritis (75% of glomeruli affected) and the linear Ig deposition pattern on immunofluorescence are consistent with Goodpasture's disease. Although IgA nephropathy can occasionally present with crescents and linear Ig deposition, it usually shows a mesangial IgA deposition pattern.

Membranous glomerulonephritis would typically have a more gradual onset, with subnephrotic proteinuria and less pronounced hematuria, without a linear Ig deposition pattern on immunofluorescence.

Plasmapheresis is indeed indicated in Goodpasture's disease due to the presence of circulating anti-GBM antibodies, which can be targeted by this treatment.
