Based on the presented symptoms and radiographic findings of joint space narrowing, sclerosis, and osteophytes, the most likely diagnosis is osteoarthritis (OA) of the hip. The radiographic changes are consistent with OA, a degenerative joint disease common in older adults.

Although a hip MRI could provide additional information, it is not necessary for diagnosis as the radiographic findings are sufficient.

Surgical intervention, such as hip prosthesis placement, is typically reserved for advanced stages of OA when conservative treatments like paracetamol, weak opioids, or a combination of both are insufficient to manage pain and maintain functionality.

While opioids may provide some pain relief, they do not halt the progression of OA, and their long-term use should be cautiously considered
