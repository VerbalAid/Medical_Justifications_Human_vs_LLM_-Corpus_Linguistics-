Based on the symptoms presented – general malaise, progressive weakness, pallor, anemia (low hemoglobin, low MCV), thrombocytopenia (low platelets), leukopenia (low white blood cells), elevated LDH, and undetectable haptoglobin – a diagnosis of hemolytic anemia is strongly suggested. Although the direct Coombs test is typically used to diagnose immune hemolytic anemia, the anemia in this case appears to be microcytic, which is atypical for Coombs-positive hemolytic anemia.
