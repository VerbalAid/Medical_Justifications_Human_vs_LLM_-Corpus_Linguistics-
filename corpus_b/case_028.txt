The most probable initial diagnosis in this case appears to be Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (GPA), previously known as Wegener's granulomatosis. The patient's symptoms of asthenia, febrile fever, nasal obstruction, mucus emission, and clots, along with the right eye pain and proptosis, suggest a systemic small-vessel vasculitis. The erythematous mucosa in the nostrils, microhematuria, and proteinuria support this diagnosis, as they are common findings in GPA. Although Eosinophilic granulomatosis with polyangiitis (EGPA) may also present with nasal symptoms and proteinuria, eosinophilia is typically a feature, which is not evident in this case
