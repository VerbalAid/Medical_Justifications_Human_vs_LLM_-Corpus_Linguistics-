The most likely diagnosis in this case is a Fracture-dislocation of Monteggia, given the fork dorsum deformity of the wrist and functional impotence, which is a loss of forearm rotation due to radial head dislocation. While a Radial head fracture can cause similar symptoms, the combination of deformity and functional impotence suggests a more complex injury such as a Monteggia fracture. Epiphysiolysis of the distal radius, Fracture in the green stem of ulna, and other common differentials like scaphoid fracture or distal radius fracture, are less likely given the lack of specific symptoms related to these conditions in the vignette.
