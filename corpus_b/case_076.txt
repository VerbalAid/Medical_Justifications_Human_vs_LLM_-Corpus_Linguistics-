Based on the presented symptoms and EEG findings, the most likely diagnosis appears to be Lennox-Gastaut Syndrome (LGS). The patient's history of absences, recent onset of generalized tonic-clonic seizures, and violent upper limb jerking (myoclonic seizures) are consistent with LGS, particularly the nocturnal worsening of symptoms, which is a characteristic feature of this syndrome. The EEG showing acute polyspike discharges at 6 cycles/second further supports this diagnosis, as this pattern is common in LGS.
