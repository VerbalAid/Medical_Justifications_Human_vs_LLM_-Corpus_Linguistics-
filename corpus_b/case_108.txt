Based on the clinical presentation and radiographic findings of joint space narrowing, sclerosis, and osteophytes, the most likely diagnosis is osteoarthritis (OA) of the hip. While the evolution of OA can be variable, the initial treatment strategy typically involves symptomatic management. Surgical intervention, such as a hip prosthesis (option 1), is usually indicated when conservative measures fail to provide adequate pain relief and functional impairment becomes significant.
