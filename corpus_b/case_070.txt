Given the severe bone marrow aplasia, the primary goal is to restore hematopoiesis and immune function as quickly as possible. While supportive care with periodic transfusions and antibiotics (Option 1) may provide temporary relief, it does not address the underlying cause. Androgens and platelet transfusions (Option 2) might improve red blood cell and platelet levels, respectively, but they do not address the bone marrow failure.

Allogeneic bone marrow transplantation from an HLA-identical sibling (Option 3) is a viable option, especially when a suitable donor is available. However, the scarcity of matched donors limits its application. Autologous bone marrow transplantation to avoid rejection (Option 4) is generally not the first-line treatment for bone marrow failure syndromes due to the risk of relapse and the need for myeloablation.
