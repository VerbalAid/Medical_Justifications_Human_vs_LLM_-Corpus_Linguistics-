Given the patient's presentation of severe neurological symptoms, such as tremor, ataxia, dysarthria, myoclonus, and fasciculations, along with lithium toxicity (Lithemia 4.1 mEq/L exceeding the toxicity threshold of 1.6 mEq/L), the most indicated therapy would be hemodialysis. This is due to its efficiency in rapidly removing lithium, which is poorly excreted by the kidneys, thus addressing the underlying cause of the neurological symptoms. Other options like aminophylline, activated charcoal, and forced diuresis may have limited efficacy in this severe case of lithium toxicity.
