Based on the presented vignette, the most likely diagnosis is Exudative Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD). The key indicators are the presence of abundant hard exudates, localized neurosensory retinal detachment, and the mention of metamorphopsia (visual distortions) - all of which are characteristic of exudative AMD. In exudative AMD, choroidal neovascularization leads to the accumulation of extravasated lipid and protein, manifesting as hard exudates, and can result in retinal detachment and distorted vision.

While other diagnoses, such as Acute Posterior Vitreous Detachment (APVD) and Central Retinal Artery Obstruction (CRAO), may cause similar symptoms, the absence of typical features like vitreous cells or a sudden onset of vision loss argues against these diagnoses.
