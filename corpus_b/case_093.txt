Diagnosis: Hydatidiform Mole (complete or partial)

Explanation:

The patient's history of spontaneous abortion at 7 weeks of gestation, coupled with the pathological findings of molar disease, suggests a hydatidiform mole. This is a rare gestational trophoblastic disorder characterized by an abnormal growth of trophoblastic tissue in the uterus, leading to an abnormal pregnancy that cannot progress beyond the first trimester.

The anatomopathological study of the abortive remains indicates a molar pregnancy, a pathological hallmark of the condition. This diagnosis has important implications for future reproductive plans and follow-up.

Option 1 is likely correct as the risk of recurrence is indeed elevated, with a reported 50% risk of a new molar gestation in a future pregnancy.
