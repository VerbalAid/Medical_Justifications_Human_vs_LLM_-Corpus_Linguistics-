In pregnant women, Escherichia coli is the most frequent causative agent in urinary tract infections (UTIs). This is due to its ability to adhere to the urinary tract epithelium, leading to recurrent infections. While other organisms like Enterococcus faecalis, Streptococcus agalactiae, Proteus mirabilis, and Staphylococcus saprophyticus can also cause UTIs, E. coli is the most prevalent.
