This presentation is consistent with a hemimedullary syndrome, specifically the Dejerine-Roussy type. The symptoms suggest a lesion affecting the contralateral spinothalamic tract (left leg thermoalgesic deficit) and ipsilateral dorsal column (right leg vibratory and positional deficits). Additionally, the right leg clumsiness and loss of distal strength, along with the extensor plantar response, point towards a corticospinal tract involvement on the same side (right). This pattern of findings is characteristic of a hemimedullary lesion, distinguishing it from the centromedullary syringomyelia which primarily involves the central medulla and presents differently. The other options do not adequately explain the observed pattern of symptoms.
