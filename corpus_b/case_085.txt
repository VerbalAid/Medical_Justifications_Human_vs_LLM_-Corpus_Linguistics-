The most likely diagnosis in this case is post-surgical endophthalmitis (1). The patient is a middle-aged individual with a history of cataract surgery two years ago, presenting with a sudden and painless loss of vision in the affected eye. This symptomatology is consistent with the acute onset of visual loss, which is a key feature of endophthalmitis.

Post-surgical endophthalmitis is an inflammatory condition that can develop following intraocular surgery, such as cataract surgery. It is caused by the introduction of bacteria, fungi, or viruses into the eye during surgery, which can lead to an infection within the vitreous humor. Symptoms typically present within the first week post-surgery but can occasionally develop later.
