In this clinical case, a 20-year-old patient has sustained a middle third facial fracture, specifically involving the orbito-malar region, as a result of a bicycle accident. This type of fracture is commonly associated with ocular trauma due to the proximity of the orbit to the affected area.

Among the listed options, the most likely complication is Enophthalmos (Option 4). This is a condition characterized by the displacement of the eyeball posteriorly, leading to a sunken appearance of the eye. In the context of a facial fracture, the displacement of the orbital bones can cause ocular displacement, leading to enophthalmos.

While Temporomandibular ankylosis (Option 1) and Dental malocclusion (Option 2) are possible complications of facial trauma, they are not directly related to the orbito-malar fracture in this case. Temporomandibular ankylosis is a fusion of the temporomandibular joint, which is not typically a complication of orbital fractures. Dental malocclusion, on the other hand, is a misalignment of the teeth and is not directly caused by orbital fractures, although dental injuries can occur during facial trauma.
