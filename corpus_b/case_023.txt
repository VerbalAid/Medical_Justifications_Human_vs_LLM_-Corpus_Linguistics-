The diagnosis most supported by the vignette is Q Fever, caused by the bacterium Coxiella burnetii. This zoonotic disease is transmitted by ticks, as well as contaminated meat and milk products (Option 1). Q Fever often presents with flu-like symptoms such as fever, headache, myalgia, and non-productive cough, which can be mistaken for influenza (Option 1). In the acute form, chest pain and bilateral pulmonary infiltrates in lower fields on chest X-ray are common (as observed in this case) (Option 2 is true, but it's not the main focus). Q Fever serology typically shows elevated titers of antibodies against phase II antigens, as observed in this case (Option 5). While antibodies against phase I antigens can be detected in chronic Q Fever, they are not usually elevated in the acute form (Option 3 is incorrect).
