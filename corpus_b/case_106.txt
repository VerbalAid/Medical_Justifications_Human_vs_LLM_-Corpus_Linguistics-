The most useful test to confirm the diagnosis in this case is likely 5- PTHrP. The patient's significantly low calcium level and elevated PTH, combined with a family history of hyperparathyroidism and persistent hypercalcemia post-surgery in affected relatives, suggest a possible primary hyperparathyroidism (PHPT) with a parathyroid hormone-related peptide (PTHrP) producing tumor. PTHrP can cause PHPT-like symptoms and resist normal PTH regulation, which may explain the persistent hypercalcemia in the patient's family members. The other tests, while potentially useful in other contexts, are less directly informative in this case.
