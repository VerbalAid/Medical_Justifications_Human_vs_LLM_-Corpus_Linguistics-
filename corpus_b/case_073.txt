Given the patient's chronic heart failure, prominent v waves in the jugular venous pulse, and a holosystolic murmur heard over the xiphoid appendage that is accentuated with deep inspiration, the most likely valvulopathy is Tricuspid Insufficiency (Option 3).

The prominent v waves suggest right atrial pressure overload, which can occur with tricuspid regurgitation due to the increased right ventricular pressure from the backflow of blood during systole. The holosystolic murmur localized to the xiphoid appendage, which is the area where the tricuspid valve is located, further supports this diagnosis.
