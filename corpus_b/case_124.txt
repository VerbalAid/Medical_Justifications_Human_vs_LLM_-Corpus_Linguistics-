Considering the symptoms and the isolated Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV), supplemental oxygen (3) and nebulized salbutamol (2) are indicated to support the infant's oxygenation and alleviate the wheezing, respectively. Intravenous corticosteroids (4) are usually reserved for severe or complicated RSV cases, such as pneumonia or bronchiolitis with hypoxemia or hypercarbia that doesn't respond to other treatments. Oral Rivabirin (1) is not typically used in the treatment of RSV due to limited efficacy and potential toxicity. However, it may be considered in high-risk infants, such as those with chronic lung disease or immunodeficiency.
