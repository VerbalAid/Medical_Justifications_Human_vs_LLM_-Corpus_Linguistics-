Diagnosis 1, Urticaria, is a strong consideration due to the patient's generalized pruritic eruption with erythematous-edematous plaques. The absence of desquamation and rapid resolution within 24 hours aligns with this diagnosis. The patient's age and the fact that mucous membranes are spared also support this, as these symptoms are uncommon in other listed options like Rubella (which typically affects children under 5) or Staphylococcal toxic shock, which often presents with fever, hypotension, and signs of multiorgan dysfunction.

Toxicoderma, or spider bite, is a possibility given the annular morphology, but the rapid resolution and the generalized nature of the eruption are unusual for this diagnosis.
