The false statement regarding the diagnosis of ITP is number 1: The presence of lymphadenopathy or splenomegaly in the physical examination does not suggest a different diagnosis of ITP. While these findings can occur in other conditions causing thrombocytopenia, they are not exclusionary criteria for ITP, as the disease primarily manifests through immune-mediated platelet destruction. The other statements are generally accurate in the context of ITP: bone marrow analysis may show decreased megakaryocytes, complete blood count often shows isolated thrombocytopenia without large platelets (although mild macrothrombocytopenia can occur), the diagnosis can be established based on clinical presentation and laboratory findings, and determining antiplatelet antibodies can support but not definitively establish the diagnosis.
