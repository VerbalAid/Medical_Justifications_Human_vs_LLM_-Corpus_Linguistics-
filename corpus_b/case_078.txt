Refeeding syndrome is a complex metabolic disorder that can occur in patients who are malnourished and then suddenly start receiving nutritional support. The condition typically develops within the first 5 days of feeding and is characterized by electrolyte shifts, fluid retention, and metabolic disturbances.

The risk factors for a patient presenting with refeeding syndrome include previous caloric malnutrition, anorexia nervosa, prolonged vomiting and diarrhea, and the elderly. In this case, non-morbid obesity is NOT considered a risk factor for a patient presenting with this condition, as it does not necessarily imply malnutrition or rapid nutritional rehabilitation.
