Given the case of a 25-year-old woman who is a heterozygous factor V Leiden carrier with no history of thrombotic phenomena, the key concern is the potential risk of venous thromboembolism (VTE) during pregnancy.

While pregnancy does induce a prothrombotic state (Option 1), the risk of VTE is generally low in women who are heterozygous for factor V Leiden. However, during pregnancy, the risk does increase slightly, particularly in the immediate puerperium due to hormonal changes and postpartum stress.

Option 2, treatment with low molecular weight heparin (LMWH) at prophylactic doses, is a reasonable approach in this case. LMWH can effectively reduce the risk of VTE in pregnant women with inherited thrombophilias, such as factor V Leiden. This treatment should be initiated in the immediate puerperium and can be continued optionally during pregnancy, given the increased risk in the postpartum period.

Option 3, suggesting no treatment in pregnancy and puerperium for heterozygous factor V Leiden, is not advisable. While the risk may be low in this specific case, the potential benefits of prophylactic treatment outweigh the risks, especially considering the patient's desire to become pregnant.
