The most likely diagnosis in this case is 4- Eosinophilic esophagitis. The patient's history of asthma, allergy to pollens, mites, and cat hair, coupled with the recurrent sensation of food impaction and difficulty swallowing, particularly retrosternal symptoms and the inability to swallow saliva, are all suggestive of this condition. Eosinophilic esophagitis is an allergic inflammatory disorder characterized by the accumulation of eosinophils in the esophagus, leading to symptoms of dysphagia, food impaction, and sometimes asthma.
