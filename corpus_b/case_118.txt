This case suggests a diagnosis of absence seizures, commonly associated with 3 Hz spike-wave discharges on EEG. First-line treatment would typically be with Valproate or Ethosuximide, both effective in managing absence seizures. While Carbamazepine and Phenytoin are commonly used for generalized tonic-clonic seizures and focal seizures respectively, they are not typically first-line choices for absence seizures. Gabapentin and Clonazepam are more commonly used for other types of seizures, such as neuropathic pain or myoclonic seizures respectively. Therefore, Valproate or Ethosuximide would be the most appropriate first-line elective treatment.
