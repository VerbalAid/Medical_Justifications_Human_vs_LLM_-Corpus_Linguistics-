Diagnosis: Hyper IgE syndrome (2)

The patient's history of recurrent infections, including otitis media, meningococcal meningitis, and pneumonia, coupled with his familial predisposition to infectious-related mortality, suggests an immunodeficiency disorder. The thrombopenic purpura episodes, despite negative antiplatelet antibodies and normal megakaryocytes in bone marrow, further support this suspicion.

The immunological study's findings of a decrease in T-lymphocyte subpopulations, elevated IgA and IgE, and decreased IgM and IgG at the lower limit of normal, suggest a dysregulation in immunoglobulin production and T-cell function. This pattern is consistent with Hyper IgE syndrome (HIES), also known as Job syndrome, where there is overproduction of IgE and recurrent infections.

Wiskott-Aldrich syndrome (1) is characterized by eczema, thrombocytopenia, and immunodeficiency, but it typically presents with bleeding diatheses, not recurrent infections. Transient hypogammaglobulinemia of childhood (3) is self-limiting and usually resolves by the age of 5 years, making it less likely in this case. X-linked severe combined immunodeficiency (4) is characterized by severe combined immunodeficiency, recurrent infections, and normal IgE levels, making it less consistent with the presented findings.
