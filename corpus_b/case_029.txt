In the presented case, a 75-year-old woman with a known history of soft drusen in the fundus has recently developed significant visual loss and metamorphopsia in her right eye. The onset of these symptoms over a short period, coupled with the age of the patient, suggests a progressive macular disorder.

Given the patient's history of drusen, the most probable diagnosis is **4- Senile macular degeneration (AMD)**. In AMD, drusen accumulate beneath the retina, often in the macular region, leading to progressive visual loss. The development of metamorphopsia, a distortion in the perception of shapes, is a common symptom of AMD, especially in the advanced stages of the disease.
