In this case, the presentation suggests a urinary tract infection (UTI) with pyuria, hematuria, and bacteriuria, along with a febrile illness. The Gram-negative bacilli observed in the urine culture further support this diagnosis. The absence of leukocytosis in the blood analysis, however, indicates that this is likely a low-grade infection.

Amoxicillin and its extended-spectrum derivative, Amoxicillin-clavulanic acid, are ineffective against most Gram-negative pathogens, such as E. coli, the most common causative organism in UTIs in young children. Therefore, these antibiotics are least appropriate for empirical treatment in this case.
