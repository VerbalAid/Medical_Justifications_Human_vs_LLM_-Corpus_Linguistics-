The presented case suggests a picture of a young woman with a significant weight loss, loss of appetite, and difficulties concentrating, which are characteristic features of Major Depressive Disorder (MDD). Furthermore, the patient's lack of interest in social activities, pessimistic thoughts about the future, and the absence of self-harming ideation indicate a depressive episode without the presence of psychotic features.

While Anorexia Nervosa (AN) can present with weight loss and loss of appetite, the absence of a fear of weight gain or body image disturbance argues against this diagnosis. Anxiety Disorders may also present with anxiety and difficulty concentrating but the patient's depressive symptoms and lack of specific anxiety symptoms make this less likely.

Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD) often presents with emotional instability, impulsivity, and difficulties in interpersonal relationships, but the patient's symptoms are more suggestive of a depressive disorder.
