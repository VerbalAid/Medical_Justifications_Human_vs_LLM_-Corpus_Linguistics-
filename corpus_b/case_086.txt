The histopathology findings suggest a thickened subepithelial area, epithelial atrophy, denudation, and increased intraepithelial lymphocyte density. Although these features can be seen in various inflammatory bowel diseases (IBD), the absence of ulcers or friable areas on endoscopy, combined with the characteristic histological changes, make collagenous colitis the most likely diagnosis. This condition is characterized by the presence of a thickened collagen band beneath the epithelium, which is highlighted by Masson's trichrome technique, as observed.
