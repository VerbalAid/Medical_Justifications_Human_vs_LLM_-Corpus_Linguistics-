The most appropriate treatment for this patient with a second-degree flame burn affecting 10% of the body surface, particularly the right arm, and resulting in the loss of arterial pulse in the hand, is likely to be option 3: Escharotomy or emergency decompression incisions.

This intervention is crucial for preventing compartment syndrome, which can develop due to edema caused by the burn. Compartment syndrome results from increased pressure within a confined space, restricting blood flow, and potentially leading to tissue ischemia and necrosis. Prompt escharotomy can relieve this pressure and help maintain blood flow to the affected area, thus improving the chances of limb salvage.
