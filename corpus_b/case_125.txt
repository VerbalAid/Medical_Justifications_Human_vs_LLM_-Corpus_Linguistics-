Given the patient's presentation (headache, asthenia, petechiae, elevated reticulocyte count, schistocytes on blood smear, and elevated LDH and bilirubin), along with the normal coagulation studies, the most likely diagnosis is **disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC)**. This condition is characterized by microthrombi formation and consumptive coagulopathy, leading to thrombocytopenia, hemolysis, and organ dysfunction. The schistocytes on the blood smear are consistent with microangiopathic hemolysis, a common finding in DIC.
