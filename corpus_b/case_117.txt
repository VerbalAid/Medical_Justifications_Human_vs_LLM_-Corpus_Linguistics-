Given the patient's history of pancreatic neoplasm and chemotherapy, the development of a pulmonary embolism is a concern. Among the listed tests, a Helical CT (Option 5) is most appropriate as it efficiently evaluates both the chest and the pulmonary vasculature, making it the most cost-effective test for diagnosing pulmonary embolism in this context. Alternatives such as phlebography or venous Doppler ultrasound may provide useful information, but their limited field of view and requirement for invasive procedures make CT the preferred initial choice. D-dimer may support the diagnosis but is not specific enough to confirm it in this case, and MRI is generally not the first-line imaging modality for suspected pulmonary embolism.
