The most likely diagnosis is Aspergillus fumigatus, given the patient's immunodeficiency status and the characteristic meniscus halo sign or crescentic contour on the chest X-ray/CT. This pattern is commonly associated with aspergilloma, a condition caused by Aspergillus species in immunocompromised individuals. While Streptococcus pneumoniae and Pseudomonas aeruginosa can also present as pneumonia, they typically do not produce the meniscus halo sign, and Staphylococcus aureus and Candida albicans are less common causes of pneumonia in immunodeficient patients.
